Chapter 1: Video Timing Fundamentals

Video timing is the foundation of digital video systems. From the early days of VGA to modern 8K displays, understanding how video signals are structured and timed is crucial for designing reliable video pipelines.

Video Resolution Evolution

Understanding Video Timing

Video timing consists of several components that define how pixels are transmitted and displayed. The timing signal includes active video, blanking periods, and synchronization pulses.

Timing Parameters

  • Horizontal Active: Number of pixels per line
  • Horizontal Front Porch: Pixels before sync pulse
  • Horizontal Sync: Sync pulse width in pixels
  • Horizontal Back Porch: Pixels after sync pulse
  • Vertical Active: Number of lines per frame
  • Vertical Front Porch: Lines before sync pulse
  • Vertical Sync: Sync pulse height in lines
  • Vertical Back Porch: Lines after sync pulse
